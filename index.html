Tax Credits vs. Deductions

Credits directly reduce the tax you owe dollar-for-dollar; deductions reduce your taxable income. Credits are generally more valuable.

Learn more
📊

How Tax Brackets Work

The US uses a progressive tax system — you only pay the higher rate on income within that bracket, not on all your income.

Learn more
💡

Why NOT to Want a Big Refund

A large refund means you gave the government an interest-free loan. It's better to adjust your withholding and keep more each paycheck.

🏔️ Debt Repayment Strategies
❄️ Snowball Method Motivation-based

Pay off the smallest debt first regardless of interest rate, then roll that payment to the next smallest. Builds psychological momentum through quick wins.

Pros
Quick wins boost motivation
Simple to follow
Cons
May pay more interest overall
Ignores interest rates
🔗 Learn About Snowball Method
🌊 Avalanche Method Math-optimal

Pay off the highest interest rate debt first, minimizing total interest paid. More mathematically efficient but requires more patience.

Pros
Saves more money overall
Mathematically optimal
Cons
Takes longer to see progress
Harder to stay motivated
🔗 Learn About Avalanche Method
